# Shirley's Gold Trading Enterprise Design System

## Brand Direction
- Position the brand as premium, globally connected, operationally disciplined, and high-trust.
- Balance understated luxury with institutional seriousness.
- Lead with credibility, not hype.

## Visual Language
- Use graphite, carbon, and ink as the dominant surfaces.
- Reserve metallic gold and warm amber for accents, dividers, and data emphasis.
- Use ivory and muted fog tones to create depth and quiet contrast.
- Favor a crisp grid, long horizontal lines, and large spacing over dense card-heavy layouts.

## Typography
- Use an editorial serif feel for key brand statements and oversized headings.
- Use a clean modern sans-serif feel for paragraphs, navigation, labels, and data.
- Keep paragraph widths controlled for readability and confidence.

## Motion
- Motion should feel refined and slow.
- Prefer soft reveal transitions, subtle parallax-free hover states, and low-amplitude section movement.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.

## Component Rules
- Panels may use very subtle translucency, but never glossy glassmorphism.
- Gradients must be restrained and atmospheric, not colorful.
- Icons should remain geometric and simple.
- Data and compliance modules should read as precise, not decorative.

## Content Rules
- Every trust or compliance claim must map back to the presentation content.
- Avoid startup clichés, inflated promises, or vague “world-class” language without support.
- Keep the company brand primary; position Shirley Ama Daniels as a leadership credibility layer.

## SEO and AI Discoverability
- Use semantic headings and descriptive text on every page.
- Keep key company facts in crawlable HTML, not embedded in graphics.
- Add structured data for organization, website, breadcrumbs, and leadership.
- Use explicit entity names, jurisdictions, business divisions, and service descriptions in plain factual language.

## Accessibility
- Maintain WCAG AA contrast.
- Ensure visible keyboard focus states.
- Keep navigation, CTA ordering, and section landmarks screen-reader friendly.
- Support reduced motion and text scaling.
